# MDRRMO Client Survey

## Title
**Client Evaluation Survey for MANA (Manila Advisory Network Alert)**

## Purpose
This survey is intended for the **Manila Disaster Risk Reduction and Management Office (MDRRMO)** to evaluate the MANA system as a disaster response recommendation and decision support tool. The results will be used to assess the system's functionality, usability, reliability, and operational value for disaster monitoring and response.

## ISO/IEC 25010 Mapping for Thesis Use
Use this mapping in your thesis to explain how the survey evaluates software quality based on ISO/IEC 25010.

### Section A: Functional Suitability
**ISO/IEC 25010 characteristic:** Functional Suitability

**Why it fits:**
- Measures whether the system provides the functions needed by MDRRMO
- Checks whether outputs such as classification, prioritization, and recommendations support the intended operational tasks

**Related sub-characteristics:**
- Functional completeness
- Functional correctness
- Functional appropriateness

### Section B: Usability
**ISO/IEC 25010 characteristic:** Usability

**Why it fits:**
- Measures how easy the system is to learn, understand, and use
- Evaluates whether users can quickly locate information and interact with the dashboard efficiently

**Related sub-characteristics:**
- Appropriateness recognizability
- Learnability
- Operability
- User interface aesthetics
- Accessibility

### Section C: Reliability and Timeliness
**ISO/IEC 25010 characteristic:** Reliability

**Why it fits:**
- Measures whether the system performs consistently and dependably during operations
- Evaluates whether the system can be trusted during disaster events and high-information situations

**Related sub-characteristics:**
- Maturity
- Availability
- Fault tolerance
- Recoverability

**Note for thesis writing:**
The term "timeliness" in this section may also be discussed as part of system performance in practical use, but for your survey, it is best positioned under reliability because the focus is on dependable and operationally useful outputs.

### Section D: Decision Support Value
**ISO/IEC 25010 characteristic:** Functional Suitability

**Why it fits:**
- Measures whether the system's recommendations and prioritization are appropriate for actual decision-making
- Focuses on the usefulness of system outputs in supporting MDRRMO response actions

**Related sub-characteristics:**
- Functional appropriateness
- Functional correctness

**Alternative discussion in thesis:**
You may also explain that this section reflects the practical value of the system in real operations, but its strongest formal alignment under ISO/IEC 25010 is still Functional Suitability.

### Section E: Deployment Readiness
**ISO/IEC 25010 characteristic:** Portability and Usability

**Why it fits:**
- Evaluates whether the system can be adopted in the target office environment
- Measures whether the system is ready for transition into actual MDRRMO workflows with manageable training and setup

**Related sub-characteristics:**
- Portability: Adaptability
- Usability: Learnability and Operability

**Note for thesis writing:**
Deployment readiness is not a single standalone ISO/IEC 25010 characteristic, so in your thesis you can describe this section as a practical implementation-oriented section primarily supported by Portability and Usability concepts.
